Research Overview

I work on control, protection, and hardware-aware design of power-electronic converters for renewable-rich power systems. My current research focuses on grid-forming inverter control, especially dispatchable virtual oscillator control, fault ride-through current limiting, and stability-preserving operation under severe grid disturbances.

My broader goal is to make inverter-dominated power systems more resilient, standards-compliant, and practically deployable by combining nonlinear control design, electromagnetic transient simulation, and experimental validation on high-frequency SiC converter platforms.

About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at Arizona State University. My research lies at the intersection of power electronics, power systems, and control, with an emphasis on grid-forming inverters for solar PV and future low-inertia grids.

Before and during my Ph.D., I have worked on silicon-carbide converter characterization, automotive traction-inverter related hardware, and grid-connected inverter control. I am interested in industry R&D roles where rigorous control theory meets real converter hardware.

Grid-Forming Inverter Current Limiting

dVOC · LVRT · IEEE 1547 / IEEE 2800

I develop voltage-source-preserving current limiting strategies that avoid relying on a conventional inner current loop. The work combines power-angle limiting, voltage-magnitude scaling, and sequence-aware current shaping to keep inverter currents within practical and standards-driven envelopes during grid faults.

Lossless DC-Link Protection for PV Inverters

PV systems · MPPT curtailment · EMT simulation

This work investigates inverter-side voltage-guided MPPT curtailment as a fast, non-dissipative way to prevent DC-link overvoltage during low-voltage ride-through events, while maintaining smooth recovery after fault clearing.

Asymmetric Auxiliary Resonant Commutated Pole

SiC MOSFETs · ZVT · high-frequency inverter hardware

I study soft-switching auxiliary circuit architectures that reduce switching loss in high-frequency SiC inverters while avoiding split-DC-link balancing and excessive auxiliary switch voltage stress.
